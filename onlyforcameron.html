Q4

Association: Two variables X and Y are associated if they vary together.

Correlation: Two numerical variables X and Y are correlated if they vary together in a linear way.

Causation: A variable X is a cause of a variable Y if the values taken by Y rely in any way on variable X.

The difference between association and correlation is that the two variables in correlation vary together linearly where as association is not necessarily linear.

It should be noted that correlation/association does not imply causation and vice versa.
